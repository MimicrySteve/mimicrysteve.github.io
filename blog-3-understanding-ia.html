Understanding Infinite Animation: Scarcity
Hard assets like gold, oil, dollars, and stock are naturally scarce – one way or another they are tied back to limited resources within the physical world. The exact connection can often be quite opaque causing us to sometimes forget how important this underlying assumption is. By linking back, hard assets for free get the important qualities of being rival and/or exclusive. Essentially, they are guaranteed to be scarce.

Soft assets – assets born and existing solely in digital space – do not get this automatic benefit. It is wrong and foolish to assume without these properties a soft asset can maintain a value greater than 0 for more than a short time. Going back to an ECON101 class. We just consider the supply and demand curves. If supply is infinite, equilibrium price must be $0.

Bitcoin was the first soft asset to create these properties in a meaningful way – without resorting to the whims of governments/laws/terms and conditions. It is done plainly, in the code, for all to see. But it imposes some pretty serious requirements on the system all in order to ensure these qualities. With these important properties recreated it was able to secure significant value for itself.

Infinite Animation is the first NFT soft asset to reproduce hard asset scarcity.

The most important aspect to ensuring scarcity is to produce a system where the artwork is uncopyable. We talked about that at length in our last blog post in this series.
Being uncopyable guarantees that the supply of any single artwork will remain limited to a small set of owners- probably one person. But being uncopyable is not the only condition required for scarcity. The platform needs to also ensure that a piece of artwork is unique.

A major problem with most of the NFTs currently available in the market place is that they are very low effort artwork. On the extreme end, with generative art, an artist/coder can produce tens of thousands of pieces – similar in some nature – within the course of a few hours or days. An average of no more than 30 seconds a piece! While not impossible, the conditions are very unfavorable for this artwork to remain unique when it only required 30 seconds to produce. It’s too easy a target for a copycat. Even worse it’s too easy for the creator themselves to produce similar pieces.

Infinite Animation is high effort artwork. Pieces take a few hours to a week to make and require some skill and application specific knowledge: in line with using photoshop or other artwork tools. This means that, at least in a small way, each piece of artwork represents a commitment by the artist. Artists are limited by the number of pieces they can generate in their lifetime.
