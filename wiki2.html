While not required to make beautiful IA artwork, importing external data is expected to often be a part of most projects. When used correctly, outside data can make artwork feel alive by reacting to a live stream of not yet released information.

However, this also presents an attack vector against artwork. Should attacker be able to fake the data input, they could gain access to scenes or conditions against the artist’s intent. Additionally, if the original provider of the data goes under or changes the conditions, if the artist didn’t account for such situations, it could affect the lifetime/viewability of the art.

Therefore special consideration must be taken by artists when including such data.

While initially the ability to access arbitrary external data will remain open it is from the start an intention of Mimicry and the IA software to lock-down this feature. This is mainly to protect artists from these sorts of attacks. A service must vet desirable outside data and provide a cleaned version of the data for artists to easily use. Importantly the data will be signed at all times before being presented to the artist – meaning that software can (behind the scenes) verify the signature on each individual piece of data preventing incorrect data from ever affecting artwork.

Mimicry will be working with outside vendors to provide data in a signed format – we will be maintaining these relationships and this data to ensure correct usage. However, when the community decides certain data is valuable enough to include – even when a vendor refuses to sign it themselves – then Mimicry itself will try to act as the signing service to provide verifiable correct data.

Please note this is not an oracle in the normal crypto sense. We are not providing on-chain data. We are ensuring off-chain data is signed and available for use to artists and viewers.

We are big believers in Web3 philosophies. In keeping with Web3 we don’t rely on the old system (governments, archaic legal systems) to secure the scarcity and value of our NFTs. It is done through proper software design and blockchain.

The blockchain ensures that the future of Infinite Animation can not be copied and the future is the only real (valuable) content of our NFTs. The blockchain doesn’t and can’t make any guarantees about already aired content – and it doesn’t need it! The artist/owner of a piece do maintain the rights to the historical content as dictated by their relevant governmental zone. They can take legal action in their own zone as they see fit.
RIGHTS
We intend to open source as much of this product as possible. The format, the viewer, the marketplace, and eventually even the compiler. We expect users to use our product because we are the best product in the market. We hope that our community will build with us and we vow to always keep the desires of the community as a beacon for future development of the product.
OPEN SOURCE
Our product protects itself from theft using methodical software design and the power of blockchain. However scarcity is so important for our product that we take things one step further. We want to ensure that even the artist can’t cannibalize their own work at the expense of owners. To do this - in collaboration with Christie’s and Sotheby’s- we will be running a verification service. This service does not judge a piece of art’s quality, only its uniqueness. The validation service considers the artist’s other works and as best as possible the other works in the space. While artists are obviously welcome to publish without verification - it is an open system - the verification serves as a way to distinguish pieces and artists who abide by the highest ethos of the community.
ENSURING SCARCITY
Individual tokens are simply NFT-721 tokens. There are no unique smart contracts required to release a piece. Furthermore, no user NFTs are ever held by MIMICRY.
